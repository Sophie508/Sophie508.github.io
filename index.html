About

I am Suliu Qin, currently a Research Assistant at the Singapore University of Technology and Design (SUTD), working with Rui Cao. I was trained in the Department of Computing at Xi'an Jiaotong-Liverpool University and the University of Liverpool. My research interests center on trustworthy AI systems, LLM agents, and multimodal understanding.

My recent work studies both how AI systems interpret culturally grounded content and how tool-using agents can be guarded when their actions create external side effects.

Research

AI Safety for Tool-Using Agents

Runtime authority control, indirect prompt injection defense, and evaluation of agent actions that read, write, call APIs, or operate through external tools.

Multimodal and Cross-Cultural Understanding

Benchmarks and mitigation methods for cultural gaps in meme interpretation and other multimodal reasoning settings.

Trustworthy LLM Evaluation

Practical evaluation settings that connect model behavior, benchmark design, and real-world reliability.
